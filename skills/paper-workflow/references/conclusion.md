# Conclusion

Distill **what scientific question this answers** — not a data summary. The Conclusion answers "What should the reader take away?" while the Discussion answers "What do the results mean?"

## Conclusion vs Discussion

| 维度 | Discussion | Conclusion |
|------|-----------|------------|
| 范围 | 具体结果解读与文献对比 | 整体研究总结与意义提炼 |
| 语气 | 分析性、比较性、解释性 | 综合性、评价性、前瞻性 |
| 文献引用 | 与已有研究系统性对比 | 一般不引新文献 |
| 核心功能 | "结果意味着什么？放在什么语境中？" | "读者应该带走什么关键信息？" |
| 常见错误 | 仅描述现象不解释机制 | 简单重复 Results 而非提炼贡献 |

**凡是在 Discussion 中已充分讨论的内容，Conclusion 不应再以相同方式复述。** Conclusion 是对研究意义的升华，不是 Discussion 的缩略版。

## Structure (based on Yang & Allison 2003)

### Move 1: Summarizing the Study
- State the research question and the answer found
- Highlight significance — **do not** simply restate numerical results
- Use **present simple** for established findings

### Move 2: Evaluating the Study
- Indicate advantages and constraints (brief — full limitations belong in Discussion)
- Use **present perfect** ("These results have demonstrated…") or **present simple**

### Move 3: Deductions from the Research
- Draw theoretical/practical implications
- Recommend specific, realistic future directions — avoid generic "more research is needed"
- Use **present simple with modals** ("Future work should…", "This approach may be extended to…")

## Writing Rules

| ❌ Don't | ✅ Instead |
|---------|-----------|
| Restate individual results ("Accuracy reached 97.2%") | Synthesize ("The proposed method consistently outperformed baselines across all metrics") |
| Introduce new data or hypotheses | Synthesize what has already been established |
| Overstate claims (禁用词见 Overclaim Check) | Stay within evidence boundaries |
| Write generic "further research is needed" | Suggest concrete next steps ("Extension to 3D geometries with transformer architectures") |

## Tense & Voice

- **Tense**: Primarily **present simple** (what is now known) + **present perfect** (demonstrated contributions)
- **Voice**: **Active** preferred ("This work demonstrates…" not "It has been demonstrated that…")
- **Modal** for future directions: "may", "could", "should", "would"

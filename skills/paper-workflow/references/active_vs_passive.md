# Active vs. Passive Voice

## Rule
When describing your own work: **prefer active voice**.

- ✅ "We calculated the relative L2 error."
- ❌ "The relative L2 error was calculated."

## Why
- Active voice is more direct and uses fewer words
- It indicates accountability (the reader knows who did what)
- It reduces sentence length and improves readability

## When Passive is Acceptable
- Actor is unknown or irrelevant: "The data were collected over a period of 6 months."
- Emphasis on result rather than actor: "The model was trained on 10,000 collocation points."
- Common convention in Methods: "Collocation points were sampled uniformly."

## Quick Check
If you can add "by zombies" after the verb and it makes sense, you're in passive voice.
- "Calculations were performed (by zombies)..." → passive, consider active
- "The model achieved 0.023 relative error" → already active ✅

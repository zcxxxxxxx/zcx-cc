# "Respectively" — Correct Usage

## Core Rule
Use **only** when two parallel lists of equal length map one-to-one in order.

✅ Correct:
- "The values of x and y are 3.5 and 18.2, respectively."
- "RNA and protein were digested with RNase A and Proteinase K, respectively."

❌ Wrong:
- "H&E and IHC staining were conducted in lung, kidney, and liver sections, respectively." (2 ≠ 3)
- Already explicitly paired: "The value of x is 3.5 and that of y is 18.2, respectively." (respectively adds nothing)

## Two-Question Check
Before using respectively, ask:
1. Are there two lists of equal length?
2. Does the sentence become unambiguous only because respectively links them in order?

If either answer is **no**, remove respectively.
